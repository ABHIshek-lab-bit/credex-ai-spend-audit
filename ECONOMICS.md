# Unit Economics

## Lead Value to Credex

### Assumptions (typical B2B SaaS reseller margins):
- Average customer spends $5,000/year on AI credits through Credex
- Credex margin: 10-15% (buy at 20-25% discount, sell at 15% discount)
- Average margin per customer: $500-750/year
- Customer lifetime: 2 years (startups grow or churn)
- **Lifetime Value (LTV) per customer: $1,000-1,500**

### Conservative estimate: **$1,200 LTV**

## CAC by Channel

### Organic (Reddit, Indie Hackers, HN comments)
- Time cost: 10 hours/week
- Opportunity cost: $50/hour (founder time)
- Cost per 100 users: $500
- **CAC: $5/user**

### Content (Twitter threads, LinkedIn posts, blog posts)
- Time cost: 5 hours/post
- Reach: 500-2,000 impressions per post
- Conversion: 2-5% click-through, 20% complete audit
- Cost per 100 users: $250 (time) + $0 (distribution)
- **CAC: $2.50/user**

### Email outreach (newsletter sponsorships, cold outreach)
- Cost: $0 (free tier) to $500 (paid sponsorship)
- Reach: 5,000-20,000 subscribers
- Conversion: 0.5-1% click-through, 20% complete audit
- Cost per 100 users: $500
- **CAC: $5/user**

### Paid (if we had budget)
- Google Ads: $2-5 CPC for "AI tool cost" keywords
- Conversion: 10% landing → audit complete
- **CAC: $20-50/user** (not viable at this stage)

## Conversion Funnel

### Audit Completed → Credex Consultation Booked
- High-value leads (>$500/mo savings): **30% conversion**
  - They have real pain, Credex is an obvious solution
- Low-value leads (<$100/mo savings): **5% conversion**
  - Less urgency, but some will book for future planning

### Credex Consultation → Credit Purchase
- Qualified leads (>$500/mo savings): **40% close rate**
  - They've already identified the problem, Credex just needs to show the solution
- Unqualified leads: **10% close rate**
  - Some will buy for future use or smaller amounts

### Overall Conversion Math

**High-value lead path:**
- 100 audits completed
- 20 are high-value (>$500/mo savings)
- 6 book consultation (30%)
- 2.4 purchase credits (40%)
- **2.4% audit → customer conversion**

**Low-value lead path:**
- 100 audits completed
- 80 are low-value (<$500/mo savings)
- 4 book consultation (5%)
- 0.4 purchase credits (10%)
- **0.4% audit → customer conversion**

**Combined: 2.8% audit → customer conversion**

## Profitability Threshold

### At $5 CAC and 2.8% conversion:
- Cost to acquire 100 audits: $500
- Customers generated: 2.8
- Revenue: 2.8 × $1,200 LTV = $3,360
- **Profit: $2,860 per 100 audits**
- **ROI: 572%**

This is profitable from day 1 if we hit 2.8% conversion.

### Break-even conversion rate:
- CAC: $5
- LTV: $1,200
- Break-even: $5 / $1,200 = **0.42% conversion**

We need less than 1 in 200 audits to convert to break even. This is very achievable.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

### Assumptions:
- Average customer value: $5,000/year
- Customers needed: 200
- Conversion rate: 2.8%
- Audits needed: 7,143

### Monthly targets:
- Month 1-3: 200 audits/month (learning, iterating)
- Month 4-6: 400 audits/month (scaling what works)
- Month 7-12: 600 audits/month (consistent growth)
- Month 13-18: 800 audits/month (mature channel)

**Total audits over 18 months: 10,200**
**Customers at 2.8% conversion: 286**
**ARR: $1.43M**

### Requirements:

1. **Distribution scales:** Need to go from 200 → 800 audits/month. Requires:
   - 2-3 strong organic channels (Reddit, Indie Hackers, Twitter)
   - 1-2 content pieces that rank on Google
   - 1 partnership (e.g., Cursor community, YC network)

2. **Conversion holds:** 2.8% audit → customer is achievable if:
   - Reaching the right audience (startups with real spend)
   - Audit quality is high (defensible recommendations)
   - Credex sales team follows up quickly on high-value leads

3. **Retention is strong:** $1M ARR assumes customers stay for 2 years. If churn is high:
   - Need to acquire more customers to hit target
   - Or increase average deal size (upsell to larger credits)

4. **Credex can fulfill:** 286 customers over 18 months = 16 new customers/month. Credex needs:
   - Reliable credit supply (partnerships with AI vendors)
   - Sales capacity (1-2 AEs can handle this volume)
   - Onboarding process (self-serve or white-glove)

### Risks:

- **Audit fatigue:** If everyone in the target market has already audited, growth stalls
  - Mitigation: Expand to adjacent markets (agencies, consultancies, larger enterprises)

- **Credex supply constraints:** If they can't source enough discounted credits, conversion drops
  - Mitigation: Build relationships with multiple vendors, not just one

- **Competitive response:** AI vendors lower prices, reducing savings opportunity
  - Mitigation: Pivot to "AI spend management" (ongoing optimization, not one-time audit)
